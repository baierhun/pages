## Coding Challenge: Point-Based Progression

### Overview

You will complete a set of **5 coding exercises**. Each exercise has a different difficulty level and a different point value.

You may complete the exercises in **any order**.
Points are awarded **only when the auto grader passes**.

---

### Exercises & Point Values

Exercise 1 — Intro — 1 point  
Exercise 2 — Basic — 2 points  
Exercise 3 — Intermediate — 4 points  
Exercise 4 — Advanced — 8 points  
Exercise 5 — Challenge — 16 points  

Total possible points: **31**

---

### How Points Work

* You earn the full point value of an exercise **only when all auto grader tests pass**.
* Partial credit is **not** given.
* If the auto grader does not pass, revise and resubmit until it does.
* You may skip exercises if you choose — but you can only earn points for completed, passing work.

---

---

### Strategy Tips

* Start with easier exercises to secure points quickly.
* Decide whether to attempt one large challenge (16 points) or several smaller ones.
* Manage your time wisely.

Your goal is simple: **earn as many points as you can before time runs out.**

---

### Directions

You may only use the **course platform** to complete this in-class coding challenge. You may not use any other outside resources.

This includes (but is not limited to):

* Search engines or websites outside of the course platform
* AI tools or chatbots
* Browser extensions or developer tools
* Help from other people

You may also **not** use any operating system or browser features that provide search, explanations, or automated assistance. This includes right-click options such as:

* “Search”
* “Look Up”
* “Define”
* Any pop-up help, previews, or smart suggestions built into your computer, tablet, or browser

You may not send, remove, or access any of the material from this coding challenge outside of class.

This challenge must be completed **independently and honestly**. The goal is to demonstrate your own understanding of the material we have covered in class.
