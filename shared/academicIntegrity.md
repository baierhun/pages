# **Academic Integrity and the Honor System**

*Intro to Computer Science*

---

### **💡 What Academic Integrity Means**

Academic integrity is about **honesty, fairness, and responsibility** in your work. In CS Academy, it means that your programs—shapes, clicks, key events, or if statements—reflect *your* own understanding.

You may discuss ideas with classmates, but **everything you submit must be typed by you and fully understood**.

---

### **✅ It’s OK To Work Together When…**

* You talk about **general ideas** (e.g., “How can I make the shape move when I press a key?”).

* You **plan out your approach** together (e.g., “Maybe we can check if the shape’s x-coordinate is bigger than 200.”).

* You **compare outcomes**, not code (e.g., “My rectangle moved when I clicked — did yours?”).

---

### **🚫 You May Not:**

* Look at anyone else’s code or drawings.

* Let someone else see your code.

* Type or copy anything that someone else tells you to type.

* Share screenshots or program files with others.

* Copy any code from online resources, including but not limited to, search engines like google, youtube, any artificial intelligence.

* Copy/Paste any code.

---

### **✍️ You Must:**

* **Type every line yourself**.

* **Understand and explain** what each line does.

* **Ask the teacher** if you get stuck on syntax or logic.

* **Read the CS Academy documentation** if you get stuck on syntax or logic.

---

### **⚠️ Bottom Line**

If you cannot explain *every line* of your program, you risk:

* Receiving a **zero** on the assignment, or

* Being referred for an **honor system review**.

It’s not just about making shapes move — it’s about learning how *you* did it.

---

### **💬 Example Conversations**

**❌ Inappropriate Collaboration**

**Student A:** I can’t get my circle to move when I press the arrow key. Can you show me your code?  
**Student B:** Sure, copy this `if` statement.  
**Student A:** Thanks\! I’ll just paste it and change the shape’s name.

This is *not allowed*. Student A is copying code and may not understand it. Student B is also at fault for enabling the dishonesty.

---

**✅ Appropriate Collaboration**

**Student A:** I want my circle to move when I press an arrow key. How do you check key presses?  
**Student B:** I check if the key pressed is “right” inside an if statement. Maybe you could try something similar.  
**Student A:** Oh, I see\! I’ll write my own check for my circle.

This *is allowed*. They’re sharing ideas, not copying code.

---

### **🧭 Summary**

You can:

* **Discuss ideas**

* **Ask questions**

* **Get feedback**

You cannot:

* **Copy or share code**

* **Let someone tell you what to type**

* **Submit work you don’t understand**

Following the honor system means taking pride in your learning — the process is just as important as the shapes you create.
